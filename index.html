The Fall of the Soviet Union

Explore the key events that led to the end of the Cold War in a fun and interactive timeline.

Chernobyl Disaster (1986)

The nuclear meltdown exposed the inefficiency of the Soviet system, creating distrust and economic strain.

Proxy Wars

Costly engagements in Afghanistan, Africa, and Latin America drained Soviet resources and morale.

Perestroika and Glasnost (1985-1991)

Gorbachev’s policies of economic restructuring and openness allowed dissent to grow, weakening Soviet control.

Economic Decline

The Soviet economy struggled due to stagnation, overspending on the military, and inability to compete with Western technology.

Rise of Nationalism

Various Soviet republics, like Estonia, Lithuania, and Ukraine, pushed for independence, fracturing the USSR’s unity.

Fall of the Berlin Wall (1989)

The collapse of the Berlin Wall symbolized the weakening grip of the USSR over Eastern Europe.

Dissolution of the Soviet Union (1991)

Growing internal pressure, economic decline, and nationalist movements led to the USSR’s collapse.
